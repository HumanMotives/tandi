DATA GRAVE

What?

The Data Grave is a mischievous final resting place for your unfinished audio ideas. Why let those half-baked loops rot on your hard drive and weigh on your soul? Let them go! Who knows — they might find a second life in someone else’s project.

How does it work?

  1. Pick a file and drop it into the grave.
  2. Choose its fate: Bury or Cremate.
  3. Add a personal epitaph.
  4. Let it go.

Your entry will appear on the public listing — a permanent memorial to your sad little creation.

  • Bury: Your file is submitted under our license and may be resurrected by others in their own work.
  • Cremate: Nothing is actually uploaded (pinky swear!) — you just get to enjoy the site’s dark humor without sharing anything for real.

Origins of the Data Grave

The Data Grave is a small passion project by me, Chris van der Linden — musician (Human Motives), designer, and all-round creative who loves exploring new ideas.

It started as a tongue-in-cheek experiment: a fake “graveyard” where nothing was really uploaded or deleted (though many thought it was). That misunderstanding was part of the fun — it asked, What if you truly let go of your unfinished work? How would that feel?

To my surprise, people loved it. They wanted to actually hear what others had buried, and even reuse those loops themselves. That’s how this new, resurrected version was born.

Why so serious?

The Data Grave isn’t a business, a money-making scheme, or a sneaky way to steal music. It’s my personal creative outlet — a place to share a laugh, spark inspiration, and embrace the beautiful absurdity of unfinished ideas.
