About the Data Grave

The Data Grave is a mischievous final resting place for your unfinished audio ideas. Why let half-baked loops rot on your drive and weigh on your soul? Let them go! Who knows—some may find a second life in someone else’s project.

How does it work?
  1. Pick a file and drop it into the grave.
  2. Choose its fate: Bury or Cremate.
  3. Add a personal epitaph.
  4. Commit it and let it go.

Your entry appears on the public listing—a tiny memorial to your once-promising creation.

Bury vs. Cremate
  • Bury: Your file is submitted under our usage license and may be resurrected by others in their own work.
  • Cremate: Nothing is actually uploaded (pinky swear!)—you can enjoy the dark humor without sharing anything for real.
Origins

The Data Grave is a small passion project by Chris van der Linden—musician (Human Motives), designer, and all-round creative who loves exploring new ideas.

It started as a tongue-in-cheek experiment: a fake “graveyard” where nothing was really uploaded or deleted (though many thought it was). That misunderstanding was part of the fun—it asked, What if you truly let go of your unfinished work? How would that feel?

People loved it. They wanted to hear what others had buried—and even reuse loops—so this new, resurrected version was born.

Why so serious?

The Data Grave isn’t a business or a scheme. It’s a playful creative outlet—a place to share a laugh, spark inspiration, and embrace the beautiful absurdity of unfinished ideas.
