Using The Data Grave (License)

License Information

At The Data Grave you can Bury or Cremate your unfinished music and loops. Trust us, it will feel great!


“Bury” = your file is actually uploaded and permanently released under the Data Grave Burial License (DGBL). Once buried, others can listen, download, and use your file to create something new, even commercially — within the limits of this license. When you choose to Bury, your track doesn’t rot on a lonely hard drive — it may rise again, immortalized in the projects of others.


“Cremate” = your file is not actually uploaded, is is not audible to others, and isnever covered by this license. Cremate exists so you can enjoy the site’s dark humor without offering up your work.



Data Grave Burial License (DGBL) — Summary


  • Use Freedom: Anyone may download and use buried audio only as part of a modified or derivative work, for personal or commercial projects.
  • No Resale of Original files: You may not resell, rehost, share, or redistribute the original, unmodified audio file “as‑is” (including bundling it into sample packs or libraries).
  • No Attribution Required: Credit is appreciated but not required.
  • Irrevocable: Once buried, the license for that file cannot be revoked.
  • Uploader Warranty: By burying, you confirm you own the rights to the audio and that it does not infringe any third‑party rights.

Definitions (Plain English)


  • Modified or derivative work: You meaningfully change the audio (e.g., edit, slice, pitch/tempo change, layer, remix, resample, process, or incorporate it into a larger work such as a song, film, game, podcast, or soundscape).
  • Original file: The original download exactly as provided (or trivially altered versions such as re-encodes with no creative change).

Why This License?

The spirit of The Data Grave is to let go of unfinished work. Instead of gathering dust on a hard drive, it can live on in other people’s projects, evolve into new forms, and maybe even inspire something amazing— without enabling copy‑paste reselling of the original file.

FAQ

Can I use a buried track in my commercial project?
Yes—so long as you’re using it as part of a modified or derivative work, not as an unmodified, standalone track.


Can I upload someone else’s track?
No. You must own the rights. If you see something that shouldn’t be here, please contact us for removal.


Can I redistribute the original files elsewhere?
No. You cannot rehost, resell, or bundle the raw files (including in sample packs). Link to The Data Grave instead.
